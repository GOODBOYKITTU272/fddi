# POST-PAPER ANALYSIS

## 1 · Skill Breakdown

**Section A — Analytical Ability (50 marks, 2 marks per Q)**
This is the **highest-yield section per question**. It tests pure reasoning across six sub-skills: verbal logic (statement-argument, assertion-reason, syllogisms), numerical patterns (series, odd-one-out), spatial reasoning (paper folding, mirror image, figure grouping), coding-decoding, blood-relations/direction-sense, and quantitative aptitude (time-distance, work, calendar, cubes, permutations, mixtures). FDDI's bias is clearly toward **visual-spatial reasoning** — paper folding and figure grouping appear in both Section A and Section D. A confident grasp of these alone can push you past the cut-off.

**Section B — English Comprehension & Grammar (50 marks)**
The level is roughly Class 10–12 CBSE: short passages, common idioms, error-spotting on subject-verb agreement, basic vocabulary. The section rewards **speed over depth**. Reading comprehension dominates at 5 marks; the rest is rapid pattern recognition (correct spelling, opposites, fill-in-the-blank with prepositions, para-jumbles).

**Section C — General Knowledge & Current Affairs (50 marks)**
This section punishes selective preparation. Question distribution: ~25% static GK (Constitution, geography, history), ~35% recent current affairs (last 12 months — awards, sports, ISRO, geopolitics), ~25% economy/banking/government schemes, ~15% industry-specific (footwear, textiles, design). FDDI consistently includes 2–4 questions from its **own institute history and the leather industry**.

**Section D — Management + Design Aptitude (50 marks)**
Two distinct halves with different mental modes:
- **Management half** (Q1–Q25): brand recall, banking acronyms, marketing frameworks, ethical decisions. Almost all factual recall, no calculation.
- **Design half** (Q26–Q50): pure visual reasoning. Figure grouping, paper folding, cross-section identification, painter-and-painting recognition, embedded figures, logo identification. **No theory questions.**

The design half is where M.Des aspirants can **outscore MBA aspirants**, and vice-versa for the management half. Plan accordingly based on your stream.

## 2 · Strategy to Score 80+ Percentile (160+ / 200)

### Time allocation (150 minutes total)

| Section | Time | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| A — Analytical | **45 min** | 2 marks each — every minute spent here pays double. Skip and return on hard ones. |
| B — English | **30 min** | Easy and fast; reduces time pressure on later sections. |
| C — GK | **20 min** | Pure recall — answer instantly or skip. No re-reading helps. |
| D — Mgmt + Design | **45 min** | Visual section needs careful reading of figures; management half is recall-fast. |
| Buffer / Review | **10 min** | Revisit marked questions, double-check OMR. |

### Order of attempting (recommended)

1. **Section C (GK)** first — 20 min, all easy points or quick skips. Bank score early.
2. **Section B (English)** — 30 min, RC + grammar. Builds confidence and speed.
3. **Section D — Management half** — 12 min on Q1–25. Mostly recall.
4. **Section A (Analytical)** — 45 min. Highest stakes. Lock in your double-mark territory.
5. **Section D — Design half** — 30 min on Q26–50. Visual and slower.
6. **Buffer 10 min** — review marked + OMR.

### Common traps in this exam

1. **Section A "complete the series"** — many series mix two operations (alternating × and +). Always check the difference of differences.
2. **Statement-Argument** — both arguments are strong about half the time. Resist the instinct to pick one.
3. **Reading Comprehension** — passage is dense and time-eating. Skim once, mark questions, then locate facts. Don't read passage twice.
4. **Para-jumble** — opening sentence is rarely the most general; look for the sentence that introduces a *new* idea (Sentence "C" in many cases).
5. **GK on FDDI/footwear** — there are usually 2–4 such questions hidden in Section C and Management half. Memorise: FDDI Act of Parliament 2017, CLRI Chennai, "I love my shoes" = Bata, "Just Do It" = Nike, "Three Stripes" = Adidas, "Swoosh" = Nike, KYC = Know Your Customer, RBI established 1935.
6. **Painter identification** — only ~8 paintings appear repeatedly: Mona Lisa (Leonardo), Last Supper (Leonardo), Starry Night (Van Gogh), Guernica (Picasso), The Scream (Munch), Persistence of Memory (Dalí), Girl with Pearl Earring (Vermeer), Sunflowers (Van Gogh).
7. **Cross-section** — onion (concentric rings), capsicum (3-chamber star), tomato (locules with seeds), cucumber (seeded oval), lady-finger (5-point star). Memorise this set.
8. **Paper folding** — count the number of layers AFTER folding, then multiply the cuts. Two folds = 4 layers = each cut produces 4 holes.

### How toppers think differently

- **They mark and move.** A topper never spends more than 90 seconds on a 1-mark question on the first pass.
- **They batch by confidence, not by paper order.** Do all your 100% confident answers first across all sections, then return for 60-80% confidence.
- **They invest disproportionately in Section A.** If you raise A from 30/50 to 40/50, that's a 20-mark swing — the difference between not qualifying and topping.
- **They never skip OMR sanity checks.** Every 25 questions, check that the answer in the OMR row matches the question number.
- **They don't fight a stuck question.** If the figure is unclear, eliminate two options and guess intelligently from the remaining two — expected value is positive in unmarked-negative-marking exams.
